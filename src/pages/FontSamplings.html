Designing with Active Hierarchy: Fonts and Contrast

The active role of font contrast in user interface design.

In this actual website example, we have activated the modern pairing of **Poppins** and **Lato**. This combination excels because it draws a clear distinction between the geometric punch of headlines and the warm, accessible clarity required for functional navigation. By applying one font specifically for **larger header text** and the other for **menu and body text**, we establish strong typographic hierarchy that improves readability and enhances the overall user experience.
